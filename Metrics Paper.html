Data Description & Key Variables¶

and runs from 1950-2022. From the Penn World dataset, the variables of interest are CGDPO, POP, AVH, CSH_C, and YEAR. The CGDPO variable is a measure of country-level GDP in terms of economic production and output. The POP variable is the

aggregate population of the countries in question, for each year. The AVH variable is the average hours worked y members of the labor forced reported for each year by country. The CSH_C variable is a measure of household consumption as a part of

GDP that year, reported in the millions of dollars. The POP and GGDPO features are of particular use because I will be using them to construct the Per Capita GDP figure in the analysis below.

The year variable is just the year of the observation in question. Below, you will find a table below inclduing all Penn world variables and their descriptive statistics.

Methodology & Analytical Framework¶

The method used to infer casuality in this report will be the use of Ordinary Least Squares Estimation (OLS), with a Difference-In-Difference framework (DiD). Any and all use of reobust standard errors will use the "HC1" method of aggration as to produce output similar to that of Stata and other commercial statistical software. I will me using the Statsmodels library (SMF), to implement these techniques.

Difference-in-Difference is a estimation model that utilizes Dummy variables to indicate treatment and control groups, and being pre/post treatment. The model also uses an interaction term to show the combind effect of being in the treatment group post treatment to estimate the effects. For this report, The United States will be the treatment group, while the United Kingdom will be the control group.

Also of note, the crime data is standardized to the per 100,000 level while our Penn World data is not standardized and is reported in the millions. Before causal analysis begins, I will be standardizing the Penn World data. For the graphical analysis, I will be using a mixture of the Seaborn (SNS) and Matplotlib (plt) libraries.

The reason for the addition of csh_c may not be immediately obvious. The inclusion of the csh_c variable is an attmept to capture the gap between the broad per cpaita GDP peasure (pc_gdp), and the real world of effects it may have which would be reflecte in an increase, or decrease, in household consumption.

Model Limitations & Further Research¶

The Models above are a rough estimation of the effects of the 1994 Crime Bill and it's effects on violent crime. While a more grainular picture would have been prefer, so that causuality could be determined, these rudimentary associations will

surve as a jump-off point for further. formally improved research. This research can be improved by findind more grainular crime data inlarge metro areas such as Los Angeles, Chicago, New York and Boston. The addition of the metrics would allow for

more constrast and balance between the the more rural areas in the data. Furhtermore, the addition of the metro area data would allow for estimation using an "Intensity of Treatment" design, allowing us to drill down and see what effects did the

laws have on specific, high crime areas. Further still, in contrast, we could take a more macro apporach and aggregate country-level data across the world for every country present in the Penn-world data, allowing for a large, country-level

Difference-in-Difference estimation with many countrols groups. An additional limitation of the data and models above includes the swithcing of the violent crime stat in the regression with the per capita GDP figures. my original goal was to

estimate the effects of crime on per capita gdp with the understandind that, crime has a depressive effect on economic figures. The reason for the switch was due ot the nature of hte underlying data. I simply have a better picture of the economic

factors than I do the crime factors. My understand is that, to use htme in their origianl order may lead to spurious correlation and misleading results. To avoid this, I sought to explain the rough picture with finer details, rather than, explain

the detailed picture with rough details.
